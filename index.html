Read this: Ecologically relevant, dynamic and functional traits, such as taxon specific pigment composition of phytoplankton as a determinant of primary production and the taxon-specific composition of essential lipids (in particular fatty acids) in the phytoplankton community affected plankton community dynamics, e.g. the food quality dependent trophic transfer efficiency to zooplankton in “wild nature”. We were able to demonstrate that experimental reductions in phytoplankton diversity resulted also in a loss of functional traits, which directly determined changes in phytoplankton performance. Altered pigment composition (CHEMTAX analyses, Ilic et al. submitted to Limnol. Oceanogr. Methods, Fig. 2) resulted in different photosynthetic activity and spectral light use efficiency (Gollnisch et al. 2017, in prep. Fig. 3). Diversity reduced phytoplankton communities showed lower light use efficiencies, mainly in wave length bands were algal group specific accessory pigments absorb light. Further, the phytoplanktons composition in fatty acids essential to zooplankton nutrition was found to depend strongly on the biodiversity of the phytoplankton community (Fig. 4 A, Stockenreiter et al. in prep.). With decreasing diversity in phytoplankton, we detected lower amounts total PUFA content and the occurrence of certain essential fatty acids. 3) We further demonstrated that our experimental shifts in the diversity of functional traits of natural phytoplankton (in our case pigments and fatty acids) had consequences for composition: Lower zooplankton content of phytoplankton diversity related PUFAs was linked to lower growth of cladocerans, resulting in shifts of the copepod : cladocera ratio in the zooplankton. These shifts were only correlated with the nutritional complementarity phytoplankton communities of for zooplankton and independent of changes in phytoplankton biomass (Fig. 4 B,C). This had so far only been found for laboratory studies with either single species or very low diversity communities (Brzezinski and von Elert 2007; von Elert 2004). The resulting zooplankton (consumer) community shifts could be observed in zooplankton biomass, composition and diversity.
